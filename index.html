YAN Mandate

The Youth Advocate Network (YAN) stands as a testament to the power of unity, equipping and mobilizing young leaders to champion critical issues for a more inclusive and sustainable Rwanda.

Established in 2024 under Rwandan law, the Youth Advocate Network (YAN) is more than just an organization; it's a vital umbrella for a diverse array of youth‚Äëled initiatives, movements, NGOs, and associations.

Headquartered in Kigali, YAN serves as a central hub, connecting passionate young advocates across Rwanda and extending its influence regionally and globally. It was born from the recognition that while individual youth efforts are impactful, a unified front can achieve far greater systemic change.

About Youth Advocates Network

Youth Advocates Network (YAN) is a network established in 2020 by youth-led and youth-serving organizations. The network strives to empower and amplify youth voices and initiatives so they can meaningfully participate in decision‚Äëmaking spaces.

According to its founding narrative, YAN emerged after a training offered by UNICEF Rwanda, the ‚ÄúYouth Advocacy Guide (YAG)‚Äù, which brought together young leaders across Rwanda. Through that training, participants gained advocacy skills, communication and stakeholder engagement know‚Äëhow, and a broader network of peers and mentors.

The Network is anchored on a philosophy that youth have gold in them and the power to decide their life locus and champion change for a better world free from any form of discrimination, harassment, and violence, and that embraces diversity.

Strategic Orientation

  • Strengthening youth voice and agency in societal issues.
  • Promoting solidarity and collaboration among youth‚Äëled organizations.
  • Facilitating capacity building, advocacy, and community‚Äëlevel impact through collective action.
  • Encouraging sustainable, youth‚Äëdriven development and social behavior change across sectors.

Context & Rationale

Rwanda, like many African countries, has a vibrant but fragmented ecosystem of youth‚Äëled organizations (covering health, education, social justice, climate, inclusion, etc.). However, these organizations often face structural limitations: lack of formal capacity in project design and management, inadequate financial and administrative skills, weak governance, and insufficient tools for sustainability.

Nevertheless, given the complexity of issues (nutrition, health, mental health, social behavior, inclusion, rights) and the unpredictability of funding or external support, many youth‚Äëled organizations remain vulnerable, especially in times of resource scarcity, political shifts, or increased demand.
